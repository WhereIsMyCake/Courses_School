    Introduction of English Stylistics¶

    • A branch of linguistic studies
    • Usually with a literary concern

    What are we talking about when we talk about style?¶

    • Reading experience
    • First impression
    • Analysis and insights
    • Final evaluation

    Stylistic Features¶

    1. Phonological features: sounds and sound patterns

    2. Lexical features: word-formation patterns

    3. Semantic features: choice of words and expressions

    4. Syntactic features: sentence types and structure

    5. Other features: discourse, visual, etc.

    Lexical Features 词汇¶

    Word Formation 构词法¶

    Derivation 派生¶

    1. Affixation 加词缀

      • prefix 前缀
      • suffix 后缀
      • infix 中缀（英语很少出现）

      e.g. - happy -> unhappy - happy -> happiness

    2. *Zero derivation 零派生

      e.g. - google -> to google - record -> record

    Compounding 复合¶

    1. n + n

      e.g. livestream, screen fatigue

    2. adj + n

      e.g. smartphone

    3. v + adv

      e.g. drive-through

    4. n + v

      e.g. humblebragging, brain rot

    Blending 拼缀¶

    e.g. brunch, smog, motel, brexit, podcast, blog, vlog, hangry

    Clipping 缩减¶

    主要是口语和非正式用法

    e.g. math, bio, prof, pre

    Abbreviation 缩略¶

    1. Initialism

      e.g. ATM, BBC, USA

    2. Acronym

      e.g. NASA, UNESCO, AIDS

    Coinage 生造词¶

    e.g. Google, Kodak, Quark

    Borrowing and Calque 借词与借译¶

    1. Borrowing 完整的借用

      e.g. sofa -> 沙发， 功夫 -> kung fu, 豆腐 -> tofu

    2. Calque 逐字翻译

      e.g. bluetooth -> 蓝牙, second-hand -> 二手, 纸老虎 -> paper tiger

    Rhetorical Devices 修辞¶

    关于修辞:

    A linguistic tool that employs a particular type of sentence structure, sound, or pattern of meaning in order to evoke a particular reaction from an audiance.

    效果：dramatic, persuasive, humorous, expressive, etc.

    Simile 明喻¶

    直接比较两个事物，一般有比较词

    e.g. As You Like It, Shakespeare

    Metaphor 暗喻¶

    直接比较两个事物，一般没有比较词

    e.g.

    Metonymy 借喻¶

    用相关联的事物指代另一个事物

    e.g. - Let me give you a hand. - The oval office is busy at work. - The pen is mightier than the sword.

    Synecdoche 提喻¶

    用某个事物的部分指代整体，或者用某个事物的整体指代部分

    e.g. - Many hands make light work. - Here come two black suits. - England beats Sweden in the last football match.

    Hyperbole 夸张¶

    Overstatement and exaggeration

    e.g. - This bag weighs a ton. - I am dying of thirst.

    Litotes 反叙、曲言¶

    委婉的说法，一般是双重否定表肯定

    e.g. - Your proposal is not unreasonable. - It was no small challenge. - Mmm, not exactly delicious.

    Parallelism 排比¶

    相同或者相似的结构重复出现，不要求严格一致

    e.g. Wilson was a Yorkshireman by birth, a salesman by trade, and insane by the age of thirty.

    Antithesis 对仗¶

    具有意义和结构上的对仗

    e.g. - Man proposes, God disposes. - > That's one small step for a man -- one giant leap for mankind.

    Oxymoron 矛盾修饰¶

    逻辑上的矛盾表述，一般包含前后矛盾的词语

    e.g. cruel kindnss, a victorious defeat

    Exercise
    • We have 20,000 hungry mouths to feed. -> synecdoche
    • The case was defended by eloquent lips. -> metonymy
    • They came to live under the same roof. -> synecdoche or metonymy
    • "Not that I loved Caeser less but that I loved Rome more." -> antithesis
    • The moon was a crescent, thin and sharp as the blade of a knife. -> simile
    • "I've told you a million times not to exaggerate!" -> hyperbole
    • "Friends, Romans, Countrymen, lend me your ears!" -> metonymy
    • Theomore is all head and no heart. -> metonymy & antithesis
    • All your perfect imperfections / Give your all to me. -> oxymoron
    • They would not love me living, so let them dread me dead. -> antithesis
    • The assassin was not unacquainted with danger. -> litotes
    • Rhaegar fought valiantly, Rhaegar fought nobly, Rhaegar fought honorably. And Rhaegar died. -> parallelism & antithesis

    Rhymes in English¶

    关于押韵：

    Repetition of the same sounds/syllable in identical or similar positions, typically at the end of words.

    效果：musical, poetic, emphatic, cohesive effects, etc.

    1. Rhyme CVC

      e.g. send & mend

    2. Alliteration CVC

      e.g. great & grow

    3. Assonance CVC

      e.g. send & bell

    4. Consonance CVC

      e.g. sent & faint

    5. Reverse Rhyme CVC

      e.g. great & graze

    6. Pararhyme CVC

      e.g. send & sound

    5. Rhyme Royal ABABBCC

    Metric Patterns in English Poetry 格律¶

    Foot 音步¶

    It is the recurring unit of rhythm, which includes a sequence of syllables and at least one stressed syllable.

    here - for stressed and u for unstressed

    1. Iambic Foot / Iamb [u -]

      e.g. away, at once

    2. Trochaic Foot / Trochee [- u]

      e.g. only, hope so

    3. Anapestic Foot / Anapest [u u -]

      e.g. intervene

    4. Dactylic Foot / Dactyl [- u u]

      e.g. happily

    Meter 韵律¶

    It is a sequence of the recurring foot in a line.

    1. Dimeter 2步
    2. Trimeter 3步
    3. Tetrameter 4步
    4. Pentameter 5步
    5. Hexameter 6步
    6. Heptameter 7步
    7. Octameter 8步
    8. ...

    e.g. - Iambic pentameter

    To strive, to seek, to find and not to yield. - Anapestic tetrameter And the sheen of their spear was like stars on the sea.

    常见形式¶

    Heroic Couplet 英雄双行体¶

    A pair of rhyming lines, each in iambic pentameter.

    e.g.

    So long as man can breathe and eyes can see, So long lives this, and this gives life to thee.

    Blank Verse 素体诗¶

    Unrhymed lines in iambic pentameter.

    Loose Sentences vs. Periodic Sentences/Nesting¶

    Sentence Branching: - Left branching

    Having witnessed his children treated disrespectfully, his wife ridiculed for her weight, and his ancient philosophy derided and ignored, the elephant threw an umbrella into the crowd. - Right branching The elephant threw an umbrella into the crowd, sending them into a panic, a panic that grew until the crowd became a roiling, violent mob bent on destruction. - Middle-branching Love, as everyone knows except those who happened to have been afflicted with it, is blind.

    1. Loose Structure right-branching

      效果：supplementary, easy, natural

    2. Periodic Structure left-branching and nesting

      效果：anticipatory, suspension-making, evasive and sophisticated
